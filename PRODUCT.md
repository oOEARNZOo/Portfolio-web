# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, and engineering team members who need to quickly evaluate Chanatip Tonngern as a junior front-end developer candidate. They arrive with limited time, scan for role fit, verify project quality, and look for reliable ways to contact the candidate.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio presents Chanatip's front-end work, engineering background, technical range, and professional readiness. Success means a visitor can understand the candidate's positioning, inspect selected projects, identify relevant technologies, and contact Chanatip without friction.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, and product-minded. The portfolio should feel modern and friendly, but still disciplined enough for real hiring contexts. It should communicate that Chanatip combines civil engineering logic with practical front-end execution, responsive UI judgment, and attention to detail.

## Anti-references

Avoid beginner portfolio tropes: excessive neon, game-like effects, crowded hero sections, oversized decorative text, unclear project cards, and animation that distracts from content. Avoid dense layouts that hide the candidate's name, role, image, or calls to action on the first screen. Avoid generic AI-looking landing page patterns that feel disconnected from a real junior developer portfolio.

## Design Principles

- Lead with employability: every major section should help a recruiter answer "is this candidate relevant?"
- Projects are the proof: screenshots, concise descriptions, tech stacks, and live/source links should remain easy to scan.
- Calm over flashy: subtle motion, clean spacing, and clear hierarchy should carry the experience.
- Engineering clarity: copy and layout should reflect precision, structure, and problem solving.
- Mobile first enough: no core information should require hover, wide screens, or fragile spacing to be usable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target strong practical accessibility: semantic HTML, descriptive alt text, visible focus states, keyboard-friendly navigation, sufficient contrast in light and dark modes, readable line lengths, and respect for `prefers-reduced-motion`. The site should stay usable on small mobile screens, tablets, desktop browsers, and for visitors who skim quickly.
